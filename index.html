3 Reasons 2026 Is the Best Year to Go Solar

01

Electricity Tariffs Rise 8–10% Every Year

Electricity prices climb 8–10% every single year, so a ₹5,000 bill today quietly becomes ₹11,000 within a decade — automatically, whether you use more power or not. Rooftop solar locks in a near-zero generation cost for the next 25 years, fully shielding you from grid rate hikes. The sooner you switch, the more years of savings you capture against ever-rising tariffs.

₹11,000 What your ₹5K bill becomes in 10 yrs
Act Now
02

Avail Government Subsidy of Up to ₹1,38,000

Take advantage of the PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana and receive government subsidies of up to ₹1,38,000* (Central Gov. Subsidy — ₹78,000 and Odisha Gov. Subsidy ₹60,000). Since subsidy schemes and benefits may change over time, applying sooner can help you maximize the available support.

₹1,38,000 Direct bank transfer subsidy
03

Easy EMI and Fast Payback

Own a solar system with low down payment, easy EMI options, and interest rates starting from 5.5% to 9% p.a. In many cases, your monthly EMI can be lower than your current electricity bill, while the system typically offers a payback period of just 3–4 years. After that, enjoy 25 years of significantly reduced electricity costs.

3–4 Yrs Typical payback period today

Disclaimer: Subsidy amount, eligibility, EMI approval, interest rates, and payback period depend on system size, customer profile, lender terms, electricity consumption, and applicable government policies.
