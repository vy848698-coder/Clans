The Basics

What Is an Off-Grid Solar System?

An off-grid solar system is a completely independent, standalone set-up that is not connected to the government or local utility grid.

It generates electricity using solar panels during daytime sunshine hours to run your home or business appliances directly. Any excess energy produced is stored in a dedicated high-efficiency battery bank, which keeps your property fully powered throughout the night or on overcast days.

1. Solar PV Modules

High-efficiency panels (Mono-PERC or Bifacial TOPCon) built to maximize output even in low light.

2. Off-Grid Solar Inverter

Built-in smart features to monitor thermal levels, loads, and prevent battery overheating.

3. Lithium Battery Storage

Provides maximum depth of discharge and rapid charging compared to traditional lead-acid batteries.

4. Solar Charge Controller

Protects batteries from overcharging and deep discharging.

5. Heavy-Duty Mounting Structure

Wind-tested structures designed to withstand harsh weather conditions.

How It Works

How Does Off-Grid Solar Work?

From a photon hitting a panel to the light switching on in your home — here is the full journey.

1

Sunlight Absorption

Premium rooftop solar panels trap solar radiation and convert the photons into Direct Current (DC) electricity.

2

Charge Management

The generated DC electricity goes directly into a Solar Charge Controller, which intelligently regulates the voltage and current flowing to the system.

3

Battery Bank Charging

The charge controller feeds the necessary power into a high-capacity Lithium-ion (LFP) battery bank, storing the excess energy for later use.

4

DC to AC Conversion

An intelligent Off-Grid Solar Inverter converts the stored DC power into Alternating Current (AC) electricity, making it perfectly safe to run all standard household and industrial appliances.

Weigh Both Sides

Pros & Cons of Off-Grid Solar

Going off-grid buys you true energy independence — but it is worth weighing both sides before you commit.

The Advantages

  • 100% Energy Autonomy. Total independence from the government utility grid and power companies.
  • Zero Outages. Provides an uninterrupted power supply as long as your battery capacity handles the load requirements.
  • Remote Accessibility. The best and often only viable solution for rural areas, farmhouses, and places with minimal or no grid infrastructure.

The Trade-offs

  • Higher Upfront Cost. Batteries account for roughly 50–60% of the entire setup cost, making initial deployment more expensive.
  • Future Replacements. Even high-grade Lithium-ion batteries will require replacement after their operational cycle life is exhausted.
  • No Subsidy Benefits. Does not qualify for government net-metering solar subsidies.

What about an off-grid solar subsidy?

To set the record straight: the Indian government does not offer a subsidy on off-grid solar systems with battery storage. The PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana subsidy (up to ₹78,000 central) applies only to grid-connected systems. To claim it, you would need to install an on-grid or hybrid system instead.

Sizing

How to Size a Battery for Off-Grid Products

The battery is the biggest line item in any off-grid system, and four design inputs drive its size.

1 day
Autonomy

The bank is sized to cover your full 24-hour load, with enough reserve to carry you until the sun returns the next morning.

80%
Depth of Discharge (DoD)

Lithium batteries can safely use about 80% of their rated capacity, so the bank is oversized to protect long-term battery health.

85%
Round-Trip Efficiency (RTE)

Some energy is lost charging and discharging. Sizing accounts for the ~15% round-trip loss so real-world output meets your need.

Split
Day – Night Load Split

Loads used after dark must come entirely from storage, so a higher night-time share means a larger battery bank.

This is why the battery in an off-grid solar product is typically 2 to 4 times larger than the battery in a hybrid solar power pack of the same system size.
