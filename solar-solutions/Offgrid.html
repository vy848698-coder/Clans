The Basics

What Is an Off-Grid Solar System?

A standalone set-up that is not connected to the utility grid — it produces electricity from sunlight, stores it in batteries, and powers your home day and night.

Every off-grid system is built from the same core building blocks. Here is what goes into one:

  • Solar panels (modules) — capture sunlight and convert it to DC electricity
  • Mounting structure — holds the panels at the right tilt and orientation
  • Batteries — store energy for use at night and on cloudy days
  • Off-grid / hybrid inverter (PCU) — converts and manages power flow
  • Charge controller — regulates how the batteries are charged
  • Balance of system — cables, combiner boxes, connectors and protection
  • Solar accessories — earthing, conduits and a lightning arrester
Is It Right For You?

When to Use an Off-Grid Solar Panel System

Going off-grid makes the most sense where the grid supply is unreliable or simply unavailable. In both cases, your home relies on battery backup for round-the-clock power. The most common use cases are:

  • Remote and rural areas with no utility grid connection at all
  • Urban and semi-urban areas that face extreme, frequent power cuts that make the grid effectively unusable

On the flip side, if you live in an area with a reliable grid connection, an on-grid solar system is usually the better choice — it is cheaper because it does not need batteries.

Under the Hood

The Components of an Off-Grid Solar System

Let’s break the system down component by component so you understand the role each one plays.

1. Solar Panels

Made of solar cells — monocrystalline (one silicon crystal), polycrystalline (multiple crystals) or bifacial (cells on front and rear). They convert sunlight to DC power; the key during installation is making sure no shadows fall on them.

2. Off-grid PV Inverter

The brain of the system. It converts DC from panels and battery into usable AC, and manages how efficiently every unit reaches your appliances. We use grid-connected (hybrid) inverters — newer tech that handles lithium batteries and lets you connect to the grid later without swapping equipment.

3. Batteries

An off-grid system lives on its batteries. During the day, panels run the house and the surplus charges the battery bank. After dark, that stored power keeps your home running. Batteries are typically 50–60% of the total system cost.

4. Charge Controller

Sits between the panels and the battery, feeding power in at the right rate and using surplus units to top up the bank. In modern systems it is usually built right into the inverter rather than being a separate box.

5. Mounting Structure

Holds everything in place. A good structure keeps panels at the right tilt — near-perpendicular to the sun for as long as possible — and is engineered to survive local wind and weather conditions.

6. Balance of System

The accessories that keep the system safe: DC & AC cables and combiner boxes, MC4 connectors, earthing strips, conduit trays and a lightning arrester.

What about an off-grid solar subsidy?

To set the record straight: the Indian government does not offer a subsidy on off-grid solar systems with battery storage. The PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana subsidy (up to ₹78,000 central) applies only to grid-connected systems. To claim it, you would need to install an on-grid or hybrid system instead.

How It Works

How Does Off-Grid Solar Work?

From a photon hitting a panel to the light switching on in your home — here is the full journey.

1

Sunlight Absorption & Electron Excitation

Photovoltaic cells in the solar panels absorb sunlight, knocking electrons loose and generating direct current (DC) electricity.

2

String Aggregation & Safety Protection

A combiner box gathers the output of multiple panel strings into a single feed and adds fuses and surge protection to keep the system safe.

3

Battery Bank Charging

The charge controller routes power into the battery bank at the right voltage and current, storing the day’s surplus for use after dark.

4

Intelligent Coordination of Controller & Inverter

The system constantly balances live generation, battery state of charge and household demand to draw power from the most efficient source.

5

DC-to-AC Conversion via the Inverter

The off-grid inverter converts stored and freshly generated DC into clean 230V AC at the right frequency for your appliances.

6

Household Power Delivery

AC power flows to your distribution board and out to every socket and light — completely independent of the grid, day and night.

Sizing

How to Size a Battery for Off-Grid Products

The battery is the biggest line item in any off-grid system, and four design inputs drive its size.

1 day
Autonomy

The bank is sized to cover your full 24-hour load, with enough reserve to carry you until the sun returns the next morning.

80%
Depth of Discharge (DoD)

Lithium batteries can safely use about 80% of their rated capacity, so the bank is oversized to protect long-term battery health.

85%
Round-Trip Efficiency (RTE)

Some energy is lost charging and discharging. Sizing accounts for the ~15% round-trip loss so real-world output meets your need.

Split
Day – Night Load Split

Loads used after dark must come entirely from storage, so a higher night-time share means a larger battery bank.

This is why the battery in an off-grid solar product is typically 2 to 4 times larger than the battery in a hybrid solar power pack of the same system size.

The Trade-offs

Advantages & Disadvantages of Off-Grid Solar

Going off-grid buys you true energy independence — but it is worth weighing both sides before you commit.

Advantages

  • Power where you need it. Run your home anywhere, even with no grid connection at all.
  • Total energy independence. No bills, no power cuts and no dependence on the utility.
  • Zero-emission power. Clean, silent generation with no diesel and no fuel cost.

Disadvantages

  • Longer payback. Off-grid breaks even in roughly 5–7 years vs 3–4 years for on-grid, because of the battery cost.
  • Battery replacement. Batteries are a recurring investment that must be replaced once they reach end of life.
  • No subsidy. Government subsidies do not apply to off-grid battery systems.
